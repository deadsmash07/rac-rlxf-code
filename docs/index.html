RLxF Workshop · ICML 2026 · Delay-Aware RLHF

A reward that arrives Δ steps late still belongs to the rollout that earned it.

Retroactive Advantage Correction (RAC) queues each pending slow reward, ages it through a decay kernel, and reinjects it as a clipped residual into the optimizer step where it finally lands — closed-form, no reward-model retraining, a two-line patch on PPO or GRPO.

The whole idea in one track. The fast reward updates the policy at the rollout step t. The slow reward — a code verifier or a judge ensemble — is computed at t but only returns at t+Δ. RAC carries it there and adds it to that step's advantage instead of dropping it.
01The training loop you already know

Standard RLHF assumes the reward is ready before the next step.

In synchronous PPO or GRPO, every rollout is scored, turned into an advantage, and applied by the optimizer — all within the same step. The reward signal and the gradient that consumes it are perfectly aligned in time.

Synchronous. The reward model returns immediately, so the advantage built from it is applied at the same step that produced the rollout. Nothing is ever in flight.
02Where production breaks the assumption

Real reward signals are slow — and the optimizer doesn't wait.

Code-execution verifiers, slow judge ensembles, and queued human review return several gradient steps after the rollout that earned them. By the time the slow reward arrives, the policy has already moved on. Drop it, and the bias accumulates with the delay Δ and with the number K of slow channels.

The cost of dropping it. A naive pipeline ignores any reward that misses its step. The error is not random noise — it is a structured, accumulating bias that scales with how late the signal is and how many slow channels you run.
fast reward — on time slow reward — in flight Δ steps dropped signal — accumulating bias
03The fix

RAC queues the late reward, ages it, and reinjects it where it lands.

Instead of dropping a slow completion, RAC buffers it in a FIFO queue. When it matures Δ steps later, RAC ages it by a decay kernel, weights it by a clipped importance ratio, and adds it as a residual δ onto that step's advantage. The base advantage estimator is untouched.

Two lines at the reward-manager interface. An O(K) queue update and a single tensor addition per step. RAC is a correction on top of the advantage, so any method that consumes the advantage — PPO, GRPO, downstream — inherits the fix unchanged.
# the reinjected residual for a slow channel maturing at step t+Δ
δi = w_age(Δ) · ρiclip · α · ( r_slow − r_fast,bl )   w_age(Δ)=exp(−Δ/τ)
Ãi = Ai + δi   # the only change to the estimator
04Why it stays honest

The leftover bias is linear in one number — and zero when the kernel is full.

RAC's cumulative correction carries a bias exactly proportional to the row-stochasticity slack η of its effective delay kernel: how much probability mass the kernel fails to reinject. Reinject everything (η = 0) and the correction is exact. At the no-delay identity kernel it reduces to V-trace, the standard clipped importance-sampling corrector.

The slack law

Bias = η · (expected slow-minus-fast residual). One slider — the slack — moves you from exact to fully degraded.

Two anchors

The contribution is the multi-channel, Δ-lagged forward injection between these two anchors — the regime production actually lives in.

A companion total-variation bound (Pinsker composed with Bretagnolle–Huber, crossover at KL* ≈ 1.6259) keeps the per-step policy shift controlled, so the correction never destabilises training.

05How we measured it

A controlled MDP for the proof, real 7B/8B reward models for the identity.

Two complementary testbeds: a tabular MDP where the optimal policy is known so bias is closed-form, and a static 7B/8B reward-distribution probe that checks RAC's core algebra holds on real signals.

Testbed A · tabular MDP

Closed-form bias, known ground truth

A 3-state × 2-action MDP with a fast channel (ground truth + Gaussian noise, σf=0.5) and K slow channels delayed Δ ∈ {1…5} steps. Delay is simulated with a FIFO buffer: a score computed at step t pops out and is reinjected at t+Δ. We measure ℓ₂ policy bias against the optimal policy, over 1000 trials per seed.

Testbed B · 7B/8B probe

The identity, on real reward distributions

N = 500 UltraFeedback prompts; responses from Llama-3-8B-Instruct; scored by a fast head (Qwen2.5-7B) and a slow oracle (Skywork-Reward-Llama-3.1-8B). A static-batch check — no PPO loop — confirms RAC's identity-kernel collapse and linear-slack relation hold to machine precision.
